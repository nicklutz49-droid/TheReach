## 1. The Big Picture

A thousand years ago, humanity was united under **the Concord of Man**. No war destroyed it — distance did. Across centuries, worlds drifted apart culturally and politically until unity became a memory; the dissolution is called **the Sundering**, by everyone, on both sides of the war. From that slow dissolution rose two superpowers, each ruling **hundreds of worlds**, now locked in a grinding frontier war, fifty-two years old and counting:

- **The Holy Terran Imperium** — a feudal mosaic of great houses ruled from terraformed Mars, beside the tomb of dead Earth. Claims the Concord never ended: the Assembly stands adjourned, and the throne holds the chamber in trust.
- **The Union of Free Systems (UFS)** — a prosperous bureaucratic-military republic of earned citizenship. Rejects the Concord as a failed model it deliberately evolved beyond.

Between and around them: client states, and at the far fringes of human space, small alien stellar nations too minor for either giant to care about beyond occasional trade and simple diplomacy.

**Travel:** Free-form warp drives — no gates, no chokepoints — but warp fails deep inside gravity wells: fleets emerge at a system's edge and burn in for days under everyone's eyes. Fronts are made by sensors and logistics, not geography. **There is no faster-than-light communication.** Information travels by courier ship or not at all — the fact that killed the Concord, and the fact both militaries are built around.

**Earth & Sol:** Earth is dead. The cause is a contested myth — the Imperium teaches martyrdom, the Union teaches mismanagement, the unfunded scholars of both suspect there was no single cause, and the truth is lost. Sol lies in the Imperium's heart; **Mars, fully terraformed, is the imperial capital.** The holiest pilgrimage in human space is a silent orbit of the dead homeworld.

**What they call each other (cold, formal):**
- Imperium → UFS: **"the Apostates"** (a religious crime — abandoning the ancestors)
- UFS → Imperium: **"the Dynasts"** (a political crime — rule by birth)

## 3. Quick Reference

**The moment:** Present day is **A.M. 1150 / Charter Year 610** — war year 52. Emperor **Alexios IV of House Palaiogos** is 97 and failing; the imperial election looms. First Director **Halima Voss**, in her third term, keeps the war exactly the size it is, and is hated for it from three directions at once.

**The skeleton of the timeline** (full table in the history file):

| When | What |
|---|---|
| c. 1,150 YBP | **The Death of Earth** — cause contested, date conventional; Year 0 of the Imperial calendar (*After Mourning*). |
| c. 950 YBP | **The Sundering** — the Last Quorum: the Concord's Assembly adjourns without quorum and never reconvenes. |
| c. 890 YBP | **The Acclamation of Mars** — the Imperium proclaimed; Kostantin I crowned in sight of dead Earth. |
| 610 YBP | **The UFS Charter** ratified at New Geneva (Charter Year 0); the Great Anathema follows. |
| 52 YBP | **The Serevan Incident** — the Reach War begins over the reading of a clock. |
| 30 YBP | **Cerulyn Deep / the Emperor's Wager** — the war's one great fleet battle cripples both navies; naval war is chess ever since. |

**The war in one paragraph:** A limited war — the limit is cold arithmetic, not treaty. Union casualties are priced in voters; Imperial commitment is priced in house treasure and standing; both prices currently say *keep the war the size it is*. UFS tech wins battles; Imperial crèches make battles not matter. Orbit is contested, fleets are precious, wars are won in the mud, and armies that lose their resupply windows don't get defeated — they wither. Halvard's World is where the war is kept.

**The twelve great houses:** Palaiogos (the purple, holds the throne) · Karolus (the war candidate) · Habsberg (the marriage-brokers) · Yamatai (the blade kept sheathed) · Rurikov (winter's landlords) · Normand (started the war) · Osmanli (the guns, and the waves that think) · Ming (merchants in crowns) · Chandragupt (the audited ocean) · Mutapa (the houses of stone) · Jorvik (the bill that comes down like weather) · Aztlan (the flower war).

## 4. Load-Bearing Mysteries

Some questions in this setting are deliberately unresolved, and stay that way — they are structural, not backlog. Each lore file ends with its own open questions; these are the ones the whole setting leans on:

- **What killed Earth.** Three accounts, no truth. Both civilizations need it dead in their particular way.
- **What the Artificer Guilds want.** The third superpower nobody counts has never picked a side, and both empires' planning assumes it never will.
- **What a Vatborn believes as he walks into the guns.** The Remembrancers' rulings on the Vatborn soul are drafted, sealed, and two hundred years deep in a drawer.
- **What House Sarkany did** — and whether the gray-draped thirteenth chair is a warning or a reservation.
- **Where Aztlan's prisoners go.** The Bestiary declines to speculate. The soldiers do not.
- **What happened to the Fortieth Legion in the Greywater** — and why the Guilds sealed the survey.
- **Where Auria Ferro went** after adjourning the government of united humanity.
- **What's under Earth's veil.**
- **What the Vatborn mean by *home*** — thirty years of interrogation margins reading, independently, "referent unclear."

## 5. House Style (for future additions)

- **Bold** coined proper terms on first use; *italics* for load-bearing asides.
- Mirrored naming between the powers is a signature (Apostates/Dynasts; the shoal/the threshold; Cerulyn Deep/the Emperor's Wager) — use it deliberately, not constantly.
- Imperial names are garbled misrememberings fossilized into orthodoxy (Palaiogos, not Palaiologos); Union language is clean, institutional, acronymic.
- Dry wit, understated horror, concrete detail; end sections on short punch sentences; every sentence earns its place.
- New files answer old questions and raise sharper ones. The mysteries above stay shut.
